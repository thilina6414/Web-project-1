YALA




Yala National Park is the most visited and second largest National Park in Srilanka.The park
consists of five blocks, two of which are now open to the public, and also adjoining parks. The
blocks have individual names such as, Ruhunu National Park and Kumana National Park or '
Yala East' for the adjoining area. It is situated in the southeast region of the country, and
lies in Southern Province and Uva Province. The park covers 979 square kilometres (378 sq
mi) and is located about 300 kilometres (190 mi) from Colombo. Yala was designated as a
wildlife sanctuary in 1900, and, along with Wilpattu was one of the first two national parks
in Sri Lanka, having been designated in 1938. The park is best known for its variety of wild
animals. It is important for the conservation of Srilankan elephants, Srilankan leopards and
aquatic birds.
