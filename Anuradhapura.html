ANURADHAPURA




The ruins of Anuradhapura are one of South Asia’s most evocative sights. The sprawling
complex contains a rich collection of archaeological and architectural wonders: enormous
dagobas, soaring brick towers, ancient pools and crumbling temples, built during
Anuradhapura’s thousand years of rule over Srilanka. Today several of the sites remain in use as
holy places and temples; frequent ceremonies give Anuradhapura a vibrancy that’s a sharp
contrast to the ambience at Polonnaruwa. Current-day Anuradhapura is a rather pleasant
albeit sprawling city. Mature trees shade the main guesthouse areas, and the main street is
orderly compared to the ugly concrete agglomerations elsewhere.
